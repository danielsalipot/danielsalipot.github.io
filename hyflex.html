Adamson University

HyFlex Learning



"The Hybrid-Flexible (HyFlex) modality is defined as a learning approach wherein some students attend face-to-face classes, while others join the same classes virtually. Teachers will then hold classes for in-person and online students at the same time. Online students are also given the choice to attend synchronous classes at their most convenient location. Supplemental asynchronous learning activities will also be provided to optimize the student learning experience. Thus, this approach offers a multi-mode of instructional delivery that ensures all students, regardless of the learning modality attended, will achieve the same learning â€˜outcomes".

Face to Face Classes

HyFlex Learning



Learning that takes place between individuals in the same physical location is referred to as "face-to-face learning," and it is one of many types of classroom training. This makes it possible for a student and an instructor to engage with one another in real time. It is the kind of educational teaching that has been around the longest. Face-to-face instruction at Adamson University takes place only in the institution's lecture halls and labs, all of which are outfitted with televisions, projectors, and cameras in order to facilitate hybrid learning.

Zoom Meetings

HyFlex Learning



The HyFlex modality conducts all of its online classroom sessions via the use of Zoom meetings. Students and instructors are able to communicate with one another even when they are physically apart while using Zoom. The instructors set up the conference room for the students to use and then give it to them. The students are provided with a recording of the meetings as well as the Chat logs so that they may review the content from the lesson.
